Executive summary

What We Think Is Happening

Apache has real trust, local equity, and customer relationships. The near-term constraint is not a lack of things to sell. It is creating more legitimate opportunities with the right buyer, then giving Jon and Victor clearer assets to turn those conversations into revenue.

Business read

Protect the core. Diversify the future.

Mailing hardware still matters because it carries equipment, meter, maintenance, supplies, and renewal value. The risk is concentration: Quadient dependence, quota pressure, and declining mail volume.

Revenue read

More good shots on goal.

The strongest near-term move is an opportunity engine: competitor mail takeovers, current-customer phone attach, transactional communications, better timing data, and targeted campaigns into current and local accounts.

Brand read

Modernize without pretending.

Apache should not try to look like a generic AI startup or national MSP. The better move is a sharper local institution: established, useful, responsive, and easy to buy from.

What we identified

The Main Findings

These are not final truths until Apache validates the data. They are the working hypotheses we would use to focus the website, campaigns, and sales assets.

01

The website should feed opportunity creation.

It should not merely list offerings. It should help Apache create more qualified conversations, support the telemarketer, and give buyers clearer reasons to meet.

02

The installed base is the leverage.

Only a minority of customers appear to buy more than one thing from Apache. Existing relationships can support phone attach, current-client drips, referral asks, supplies/service retention, and renewal timing plays.

03

Customers need education, not a catalog.

Jon was clear: buyers often do not know what these products can fix. The site and sales assets need pain examples, business cases, labor savings, and simple explanations.

04

Some lanes need validation before emphasis.

Parcel lockers have ticket size and excitement, but limited recurring value. Certified proof is useful, but should sit inside the broader transactional communications story until Apache validates demand.

05

Apache already has sales knowledge scattered everywhere.

Jon has company profiles, vendor videos, PDFs, proposals, business-case language, and objection handling spread across tools. Capturing those assets may be as important as writing new copy.

Vendor proofQuadient videos, PDFs, product sheets, and approved claims. Sales languageProposal templates, objection handling, business-case examples, and quote logic. Call realityTelemarketer notes, no-shows, stalled deals, common questions, and follow-up language. Customer proofReviews, service stories, local wins, switcher examples, and renewal moments.
Recommended direction

Build an opportunity engine, not just a website.

The website should become the front door to a sharper sales motion: protect the core mail/service base, grow recurring communication revenue, support the telemarketer, and use local trust to create more qualified conversations.

Plain-English recommendation Do not start with a broad redesign. Start by validating the offers, buyer timing, sales assets, and data needed to increase legitimate opportunities. Then build the site and campaigns around those lanes.
