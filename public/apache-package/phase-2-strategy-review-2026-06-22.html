01 / Engagement shape

What Phase 2 Is

Phase 2 is the strategy-review artifact Apache can react to before public pages are built. It should make assumptions visible, turn product categories into buyer-facing revenue motions, and identify the minimum data needed to decide where to focus.

Phase 1 / Complete

Revenue Map

Maps the business model, growth lanes, funnel constraints, brand posture, and assumptions from the first discovery call.

Phase 2 / Current

Strategy Review

Gives Jon and Victor a concrete artifact to validate: priorities, offers, quota pressure, campaigns, data needs, sales assets, and build sequence.

Phase 3 / After approval

Website + Campaign Build

Turns the approved strategy into homepage, service pages, landing pages, campaign copy, lead forms, and handoff assets.

Do not pitch this as a redesign yet

The site is the outcome. The current job is alignment: what Apache should sell harder, how to explain it, what sales material already exists, and what revenue data confirms the bet.

Primary question

If Apache wants to move from roughly 1-2 legitimate opportunities per week to 5-10, which offer lanes, customer segments, and follow-up systems should carry that increase?

02 / Offer architecture

Six Lanes To Validate

These are not finished public pages. They are proposed revenue lanes. The numbering below reflects the current Praxis read: what should create the clearest path to more qualified revenue conversations after Apache validates the assumptions.

Competitive Mail Takeovers

01

Win unhappy competitor customers when Apache can get in the room with a better local-service story.

  • Validate last 12-18 months of switcher wins.
  • Confirm best lease-window timing.
  • Build talk tracks by vertical and competitor pain.
Role Grow coreBuild later Switcher campaign

Business Phones Attach

02

Recurring revenue attached to current Apache customers who already trust the service relationship.

  • Validate current active phone base and margins.
  • Start with current customers before broad cold acquisition.
  • Identify contract-end and dissatisfaction triggers.
Role Attach offerBuild later Current-client campaign

Parcel Lockers

03

The strongest visible new-category wedge if Apache can build a channel into multifamily, GCs, property managers, HOAs, campuses, and commercial buildings.

  • Validate margin, ticket size, cycle, and support load.
  • Clarify property-manager and GC decision paths.
  • Pursue with channel partners if economics hold.
Role Expansion wedgeBuild later Property campaign

Transactional Communications

04

The bridge from mail into workflow: statements, invoices, notices, portals, email/SMS, archive, print-to-mail, and delivery proof.

  • Do not frame as direct-mail marketing first.
  • Use Quadient Impress / Distribute as proof, not as the whole story.
  • Validate deal economics and sales cycle.
  • Convert Jon's verbal business case, Quadient assets, and proposal examples into pages and tools.
Role ModernizeBuild later Business-case pages

Protect the Mail Base

05

Keep the cash engine healthy: equipment, meters, maintenance, supplies, and upgrade timing.

  • Validate renewal calendar quality.
  • Protect the four-part revenue stack: equipment, meter, maintenance, and supplies.
  • Confirm attach rates for maintenance and supplies.
  • Identify next-best-offer moments at renewal.
Role Defend baseBuild later Mail base dashboard

Certified / Proof Add-On

06

Potential compliance wedge inside transactional communications, including tools like e-Certify, but it should be validated before becoming a headline offer.

  • Validate which verticals feel urgent proof pain.
  • Gather examples Apache can safely share.
  • Bundle into transaction/compliance messaging unless demand proves standalone.
Role Validate wedgeBuild later Proof module
03 / Praxis recommendation

Where Apache Should Focus First

Apache should not have to score the strategy from scratch. Based on the discovery call, this is the current recommended order. The follow-up should validate the assumptions, especially margin, buyer access, channel fit, and urgency.

01
Core growth

Competitive Mail Takeovers

Win customers already spending with meter competitors by using Apache's local service edge.

Why it ranks here

This protects the core business while creating near-term sales conversations Apache already understands how to service.

Build firstSwitcher campaign + lease-timing list
02
Relationship leverage

Business Phones Attach

Sell recurring communications infrastructure into customers who already trust Apache.

Why it ranks here

The trust barrier is lower with current customers, and the recurring revenue profile is stronger than most hardware-only lanes.

Build secondCurrent-client attach campaign
03
Expansion wedge

Parcel Locker Channel

Use parcel lockers as Apache's most visible new-category wedge into property-driven buyers.

Why it ranks here

It may not be the best recurring lane, but it gives Apache a sharper growth story and a natural channel partnership path with property managers, GCs, HOAs, campuses, and multifamily operators.

Build thirdProperty + partner campaign
04
Strategic bridge

Transactional Communications

Statements, invoices, notices, portals, email/SMS, archive, print-to-mail, and delivery proof.

Why it ranks here

This is where Quadient Impress / Distribute fits. It could be strategically important, but Apache needs clearer packaging, economics, and buyer education before it leads the story.

Validate nextBusiness-case page + proof assets
05
Defensive system

Protect the Mail Base

Keep equipment, meters, maintenance, supplies, leases, and service retention healthy.

Why it ranks here

Mail still matters because each account can carry multiple revenue streams. It is critical infrastructure, but more of a retention and timing system than the clearest public growth headline.

Build into opsMail base timing + retention plays
06
Supporting wedge

Certified / Proof Add-On

Compliance proof, retrieval, legal defensibility, record confidence, and e-Certify-style workflows.

Why it ranks here

Useful message, but likely stronger as a module inside transactional communications than a standalone lead offer unless Apache can prove urgent demand.

07 / Suggested call flow

How To Present It

Keep the tone practical and revenue-focused. Avoid making this feel like a design critique or an operations audit. The cleanest frame is: "We mapped the business. Now we want to validate where the next build should focus."

30-Minute Review

  • 5 min: clarify Phase 2 is strategy review, not final website.
  • 8 min: review six revenue lanes and challenge the assumptions.
  • 8 min: validate the recommended priority order.
  • 5 min: confirm data access, quota pressure, and sales assets.
  • 4 min: decide what Phase 3 should build first.
