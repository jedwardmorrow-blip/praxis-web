02Strategy Directions

Four directions. One data-backed launch spine.

Each direction is a posture the campaign can adopt, with sample posts in Renie's voice. For conversion, the launch should lead with the revenue diagnostic and the data behind it, then rotate in human edge, culture, and mindset as supporting lanes.

The revenue leak you cannot see.

Most hotel leaders know there is revenue left inside the reservations conversation. What they do not have is a clean way to see where the leakage is happening. Aspire can own that problem because the assessment is grounded in 30 years of hospitality work and 250,000+ evaluations.

Why now

RevPAR pressure, rising acquisition costs, and thinner teams make this land with GMs, revenue leaders, and reservations leaders. It is practical, measurable, and directly tied to the assessment conversion path.

Converts via

"Stop guessing where revenue is leaking. Start with the diagnostic." The post gives them the reason to click.

The human edge, in the age of AI.

As technology floods hospitality, the most valuable thing in the building becomes the opposite: a real human voice a guest can feel. Renie owns the contrarian-but-true position. Use technology strategically for prep, data, and follow-up. Never automate the connection.

Why now

It is timely and shareable, but it should support the first campaign instead of replacing it. The data-backed revenue diagnostic is the sharper conversion message.

Converts via

"Measure the human edge your team has on the phone." It routes to the same diagnostic without overstating Aspire as a technology or AI company.

Why now

Differentiation in a market where every property claims great service. This is the brand-building direction.

03How It Works

One engine. Three layers of content, only one is sensitive.

The reason "let Greg run Aspire's content from Praxis" felt complicated is data sovereignty. The fix: the engine never moves Aspire's corpus, and the campaign only exports approved public language. Tap each stage to see how it works.

Stays private

Proprietary source

The 892-source world model, assessment data, methodology. Lives in Aspire's project. Greg never needs the raw corpus.

Shareable

Derived voice layer

Renie's voice profile, content pillars, approved hooks. Publishable by nature, a brand's voice is meant for the public.

Public by design

The posts

The LinkedIn output itself. The least sensitive thing in the system.

Source Aspire-side

The world model and the voice canon (keynotes, the poetry, the frameworks) sit in Aspire's own Supabase project. Nothing proprietary ever leaves.

The boundary, honored

Built by Praxis, operated by Greg, blessed by Renie, hosted in Aspire's house. Greg operates from the approved voice and campaign layer, never by exposing the raw corpus.

05The Conversion Target

Every post points to one thing: the assessment.

A short reservations sales assessment, sorted into four bands. It diagnoses how well a team turns inquiries into better buying conversations, and it doubles as the campaign's best engagement device: where is your team leaking opportunity?

Band 1
Foundation
Still taking orders. Reset from transactional to a buying environment.
Band 2
Consistency
Good, but it depends who is on shift. Make it repeatable.
Band 3
Sharpening
Selling well, leaving the premium on the table.
Band 4
Sustain & Scale
The top tier. Benchmark, certify, and coach to stay there.
One build item before launch

The funnel is mostly built: a self-serve quiz path, magic-link reports, and HubSpot sync already exist. But the default flow was event-cohort and wave-released. Cold LinkedIn traffic needs the default set to instant: complete, score, report, no wait. A small flip, not a rebuild.

08Engagement Mechanics

How we actually earn comments, likes, and reshares.

Sponsored path

Tracked URL in the post body

Thought Leader Ads amplify the original member post and do not add a separate CTA button. For sponsored versions, the direct assessment URL belongs in the post body.

Comment trigger

End on one real question

Comments are the single strongest signal. "Which of these is your team?" turns a post into a conversation.

First 90 minutes

Reply fast

Renie, or Greg on her behalf, replies to every comment in the first 60 to 90 minutes. That window compounds reach.

Organic variant

First comment or comment-to-DM

For non-sponsored posts, we can still test first-comment links or comment-to-DM mechanics. Sponsored conversion posts should stay cleaner: claim, proof, diagnostic URL.
