03Renie's Voice

It has to read as genuinely hers.

Ghostwriting a company page is forgiving. Ghostwriting a real person's first-person feed is not. The voice profile below is grounded in her keynotes, her speaking material, and her poetry, so every post sounds like Renie and not like a brand.

How she builds a point

Reframe, truth, proof, story, action

Open with a provocative question. State a blunt truth. Back it with a number. Tell a self-deprecating story where she is the example. Close on action.

Her lexicon

Plain, bold, hers

Head trash. Play full out. Shine your light. Human beings, not human doings. "Hope is not a strategy." "No one gives you stress, you give it to yourself."

Sounds like Renie

"Here is the truth no one says out loud: no one gives you stress. You hand it to yourself. I did it yesterday, guilty over a haircut. Get over it. Give to yourself first, or you have nothing left to give anyone."

Does not sound like Renie

"In today's fast-paced environment, leaders should prioritize self-care to optimize wellbeing and productivity." Hedged, no story, no edge, no number.

A reservations sales assessment. 17 questions, scored 17–68, sorted into four bands. It diagnoses how well a team turns inquiries into bookings, and reframes order-taking into a buying environment. The four bands are also the campaign's best engagement device: "which one is your team?"

One build item before launch

The funnel is mostly built: a self-serve quiz path, magic-link reports, and HubSpot sync already exist. But the default flow has been event-cohort and wave-released. Cold LinkedIn traffic needs the default set to instant: complete, score, report, no wait. A small flip, not a rebuild.

08Engagement Mechanics

How we actually earn comments, likes, and reshares.

Reach discipline

Link in the first comment

LinkedIn suppresses posts with outbound links in the body. The link goes in the first comment, or we run comment-to-DM ("comment SCORE and I'll send it"), which also captures a lead.

Comment trigger

End on one real question

Comments are the single strongest signal. "Which of these is your team?" framing turns a post into a conversation.

First 90 minutes

Reply fast

Renie, or Greg on her behalf, replies to every comment in the first 60 to 90 minutes. That window compounds reach.

Proof

Feature client wins

Auberge and others, with permission. Social proof plus their network reach.

10What Success Looks Like

Judge it on a 90-day curve, not a week-one spike.

This is a compounding authority and pipeline play, not a short-term lead firehose. Here is what good looks like at each checkpoint, so the team measures it on the right timeline and a strong program is not killed early on the wrong metric.

Day 0 to 30
Prove the Voice
Goal: it sounds unmistakably like Renie, and we post without fail.
  • Three posts a week, held consistently
  • The audience confirms it reads as her
  • Engagement baseline set: which hooks land
  • First assessment completions trickle in
Day 31 to 60
Build the Audience
Goal: grow the right followers and a pool worth retargeting.
  • Follower and comment growth among reservations, revenue, and GM leaders
  • Reshares climbing, band self-diagnostic posts outperforming
  • A retargeting pool large enough to sponsor to
  • A steady, small flow of assessment starts
Day 61 to 90
Convert and Compound
Goal: a qualified, growing trickle of leads and clear conversion data.
  • Completed assessments Greg can work as warm leads
  • A clear read on which pillars and gaps convert
  • Spend concentrating on the winners
  • Authority signals: profile views, inbound DMs
The honest measure

Impressions are a diagnostic, not a goal. The north star is compounding authority plus a qualified pipeline. Direct cold-traffic signups will be modest at first, conversion is the hardest part of this and improves as the audience warms and the funnel tightens. If month one is judged on signup volume alone, a good program gets cut before it compounds. Set that expectation with everyone before the first post runs.
