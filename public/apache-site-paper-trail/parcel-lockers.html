Who this is for

Properties that became package depots

Apartment communities, multifamily operators, student housing, and property managers whose leasing office spends real hours a day receiving, logging, storing, and handing off resident packages.

What breaks

A staffing problem in a delivery costume

  • Office staff play package clerk instead of leasing apartments
  • The storage room overflows every holiday season
  • Lost packages become resident complaints, then liability
  • Carriers drop and dash — nobody signs, nothing's logged

What happens next

A property walkthrough

Unit count, carrier volume, current handoff process — a short walkthrough gives us what we need to size it. You get a one-page business case you can put in front of ownership.

Fair questions
Who typically pays for the lockers?

Usually the property. Part of the walkthrough is a one-page business case you can put in front of ownership, built on your real package volume and staff time.

Do carriers actually use them?

Yes — carriers load lockers directly, residents get notified automatically, and every pickup is logged. The front desk leaves the chain entirely.

What about oversized packages?

Locker banks are sized to your property's real volume, with oversized compartments and an overflow plan agreed up front. That's what the walkthrough is for.
